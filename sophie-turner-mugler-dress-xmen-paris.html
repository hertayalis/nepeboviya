    Sophie Turner Wears a Mini Mugler Dress in Paris to Promote X-Men: Dark Phoenix Event

    We're not in Westeros anymore. Sophie Turner may stick to leather corsets and fur in her role of Sansa Stark on Game of Thrones, but in real life, she's crafted a style that's all her own. Her impeccable fashion sense has even earned her a coveted ambassadorship Louis Vuitton.

    18 Aug 2024, 00:00
    • Sophie Turner is on a promotional tour for the forthcoming X-Men: Dark Phoenix movie with her castmates Jessica Chastain, Michael Fassbender, and director Simon Kinberg.
    • Turner's little black dress by the French fashion house Mugler was definitely a win in our book.

    We're not in Westeros anymore.

    Sophie Turner may stick to leather corsets and fur in her role of Sansa Stark on Game of Thrones, but in real life, she's crafted a style that's all her own. Her impeccable fashion sense has even earned her a coveted ambassadorship Louis Vuitton.

    Kristy Sparow//Getty Images

    While kicking off the promotional tour for her upcoming film, X-Men: Dark Phoenix, Turner looked stunning in her sleeveless, fit-and-flare Mugler mini dress with contrast stitching. She kept the rest of her black outfit sleek and understated, and she accessorized with a pair of gold high-heeled sandals and simple hoops.

    Her gorgeous look is in part due to celebrity stylist Kate Young, who also counts Margot Robbie, Dakota Johnson, and Selena Gomez as clients. Both Young and Mugler confirmed on their respective Instagram Stories that Turner wore the French designer on the carpet. (A maxi version and a knee-length version of her dress are both available to shop right now).

    Instagram

    Turner's glam was just as effortless and sophisticated. Her wavy blonde hair was pulled up into a high bun with two strands dangling on either side of her face. Her makeup was very feminine thanks to a purple smokey eye, flirty lashes, and a glossy berry lip.

    Turner, who plays Jean Grey in the X-Men franchise, was joined by her cast mates Jessica Chastain (new character Smith), Michael Fassbender (villain Erik Lehnsherr a.k.a. Magneto), and director Simon Kinberg. The group all looked dapper in the French capital as they posed for the paparazzi outside in front of the Eiffel Tower.

    Kristy Sparow//Getty Images

    X-Men: Dark Phoenix hits theaters across the United States on June 7, 2019.
